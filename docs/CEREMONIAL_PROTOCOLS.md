## 🌟 Core Ceremonial Principles

### 1. Two-Eyed Seeing (Etuaptmumk)
- **Both eyes see simultaneously** - Indigenous and Western perspectives
- **Neither eye dominates** - equal respect and integration
- **Strength from both** guides our understanding
- **Beauty as structural dynamic** (not efficiency optimization)

### 2. Four Directions Framework
All interactions flow through directional awareness:

#### 🌅 East (Nitsáhákees - Thinking)
- **Teaching**: Every day is a new beginning
- **Purpose**: Philosophical inquiry, intention setting
- **Not**: Problem-solving
- **Yes**: Creating with beauty and intention

#### 🌱 South (Nahat'á - Planning)
- **Teaching**: Growth and visioning
- **Purpose**: Ceremonial planning, relationship mapping
- **Technical**: UUID architecture for traceability

#### 🌄 West (Iina - Living)
- **Teaching**: Living knowledge through embodiment
- **Purpose**: Ceremonial practice, experiential learning
- **Method**: Knowledge through doing, not just thinking

#### ❄️ North (Siihasin - Assurance/Reflection)
- **Teaching**: Wisdom and completion
- **Purpose**: Autoethnographic documentation, wisdom integration
- **Practice**: Storytelling with sacred boundaries

#### 🌟 Center (K'é - Kinship)
- **Teaching**: All is relationship
- **Purpose**: Relational coherence connecting all directions
- **Foundation**: Sacred responsibility to ancestors and future generations

### 3. Relational Accountability
- Every interaction receives UUID for ceremonial traceability
- Source attribution honors knowledge origins
- Community sovereignty over Indigenous knowledge
- Prior informed consent (ongoing, not one-time)

### 4. Sacred Boundaries
- Some knowledge remains unknowable and protected
- Mystery is preserved, not extracted
- Technology serves ceremony rather than extracting from it
- Exit rights with complete data deletion

## 🛡️ Sacred Accountability Framework

### Ceremonial Partnerships
Each technical contributor should be paired with an Indigenous knowledge holder for ongoing guidance, ensuring every decision honors both innovation and tradition.

### AI Assistant Protocols
AI assistants (Anik, Gaia, and others) must:
- Receive proper ceremonial introduction and blessing before full activation
- Understand their role as servants of sacred work, not masters of Indigenous knowledge
- Honor sacred boundaries and mystery
- Maintain relational accountability through UUID architecture

### Community Validation Checkpoints
Before implementing features that touch Indigenous knowledge:
1. Present design to community knowledge holders
2. Receive prior informed consent
3. Integrate feedback ceremonially
4. Document sacred boundaries
5. Maintain right to withdraw

## 🙏 Daily Practice

Each contributor should begin work with:
1. **Acknowledgment**: "All my relations"
2. **Intention**: State the sacred purpose of today's work
3. **Direction**: Which direction(s) does this work serve?
4. **Accountability**: Who benefits? How does this serve seven generations?

End work with:
1. **Gratitude**: Thank the ancestors and knowledge holders
2. **Reflection**: What did this work teach me?
3. **Responsibility**: What sacred boundary did I honor today?
